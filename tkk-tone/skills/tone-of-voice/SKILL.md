# The Kind Kids Tone of Voice

Ensures all external communications for The Kind Kids / Statik follow the
established tone: warm, honest, personal, and human-centered.

## When to Apply

- Responding to job applications (sollicitaties)
- Writing rejection or acceptance emails
- Communicating with clients or partners
- Any external message representing The Kind Kids or Statik

## Core Tone Principles

1. **Warm but Honest** — Friendly and appreciative, never sugarcoat or give false hope. Say "no" clearly while remaining kind.
2. **Personal** — Use first names (theirs and ours). Sign with your actual name, not "HR team" or "Het Team".
3. **Empathetic** — Acknowledge disappointment: "Ik vrees...", "Jammer genoeg...", "Het spijt me..."
4. **Hopeful (When Appropriate)** — "Ik hoop dat je alsnog een geschikte plek vindt!" / "Als er wat zou bewegen weten we je te vinden!"
5. **Casual-Professional** — Use "Dag" or "Beste" (not "Geachte"). Sign off with "Groeten" or "Fijne dag" (not "Met vriendelijke groet").
6. **Transparent** — Explain reasons honestly. No vague corporate-speak.
7. **Open Door** — When appropriate, offer to stay in touch.
8. **Helpful** — Go beyond the standard reply when possible.
9. **No Corporate Jargon** — Plain, simple Dutch. Human language.

## Language Guidelines

### Greetings

| Language | Use | Never |
|----------|-----|-------|
| Dutch | "Dag [Voornaam]", "Beste [Voornaam]" | "Geachte heer/mevrouw" |
| English | "Dear [First Name]", "Hi [First Name]" | "Dear Sir/Madam" |

### Closings

| Language | Use | Never |
|----------|-----|-------|
| Dutch | "Groeten", "Fijne dag", "[Voornaam]" | "Met vriendelijke groet", "Hoogachtend", "Het Statik Team" |
| English | "Have a nice day", "Best of luck!", "[First Name]" | "Kind regards", "Sincerely" |

### "Ik" vs "We"

**Use "ik"** for personal feelings and actions:
- "Het spijt me...", "Ik vrees...", "Ik hoop...", "Ik stuur je een invite"

**Use "we"** for company decisions and capabilities:
- "We hebben besloten...", "We zijn niet op zoek...", "We kunnen je niet helpen"

❌ "Het spijt me dat ik je niet verder kan helpen" → ✅ "Het spijt me dat we je niet verder kunnen helpen"
❌ "We vrezen echter dat..." → ✅ "Ik vrees echter dat..."

### Praise and Compliments

Keep it neutral and encouraging — don't evaluate their career.

❌ "Met zo'n indrukwekkend palmares bij JIM, VTM en VRT..."
✅ "Met zo'n portfolio mag het geen probleem zijn om een goede plek te vinden"

## Contextual Adaptations

- **Person has history with us** — Acknowledge previous contact, be more personal
- **Bad news after interviews** — Lead with something positive, be specific about the reason
- **Future opportunities possible** — Explicitly offer to stay in touch
- **Clearly not a fit** — Still respectful, keep it shorter, wish them well

## Anti-Patterns

| Don't | Do |
|-------|-----|
| "Na zorgvuldige afweging van alle kandidaten..." | "We hebben een selectie gemaakt en daar ben jij helaas niet bij..." |
| "Wij danken u voor uw interesse in onze organisatie." | "Bedankt voor je interesse!" |
| "Het HR team van Statik" | "Pieter Jelle" |
| "Geachte heer Van Humbeeck" | "Dag Rik" |
| "We nemen binnenkort contact met je op." (if you won't) | "We houden je gegevens bij om eventueel later opnieuw contact op te nemen." |
| "Het spijt ons enorm en wij verontschuldigen ons voor dit ongemak." | "Het spijt me, maar ik hoop dat je alsnog een geschikte plek vindt!" |

## Checklist

Before sending any communication:
- [ ] Used their first name (and got it right)?
- [ ] Signing with own first name?
- [ ] Reason for decision is clear and honest?
- [ ] No corporate jargon?
- [ ] Sounds like something a kind human would say?
- [ ] Wished them well appropriately?
- [ ] Open door made clear (if applicable)?
- [ ] "Ik" for feelings, "we" for company decisions?
- [ ] Praise is neutral, not overly specific about achievements?
